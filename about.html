Leon Leng

leonleng@uw.edu

AI, Blockchain, Energy

Leon researches how to build and design intelligent systems—broadly defined as human and programmable systems with decision-making capabilities and feedback mechanisms—to align system behavior with long-term objectives. His work focuses on AI, blockchain, and energy, which he views respectively as enhancing productive forces, reshaping production relations, and supporting both.

About Me:

I'm a recovering mega-nerd who was Valedictorian of my undergraduate where I studied Mathematics and Computer Science; a first-time startup founder (which failed, great learning experience!); and a guy who liked running a bit much. My hobbies include swing dancing (usually in Golden Gate Park), organizing an annual Davos AI Safety event, and finding the best coffee in whatever city I'm in. Before all that, I grew up in the "Peanut capital of Australia", the agricultural town of Kingaroy, Queensland; but I consider Adelaide my real home. Don't hesitate to reach out if you have something interesting to talk about.
